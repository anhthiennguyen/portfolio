My Ethos

— on living

I live in the present. What I have is temporary, and that awareness makes me more grateful and able to appreciate life as it is. I aim to deal with suffering positively, focusing on what I can control, accepting everything else, and continuing forward with resilience. I stay grounded through stillness, facing reality directly, while also allowing myself to enjoy life and find satisfaction in it.

— on knowledge

I pursue knowledge broadly and deeply across disciplines because I have a deep passion for learning. I highly value learning because I desire to grow in wisdom in order to live a more fulfilling life. I regularly reflect on my own thoughts to constantly refine the clear principles I live by. Maturity takes a lifetime to develop, and there's always more to understand.

— on people

Past loneliness taught me the value of meaningful relationships, so I step outside comfort to connect with others. I invest in them with trust and honesty, communicate directly because clarity matters to me, and I strive to be selfless meeting others where they're at and making a positive impact in other people's lives.
